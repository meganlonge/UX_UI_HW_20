Minnesota Environmental Partnership

Executed the user experience (UX)/user interface (UI) design process for an existing non-profit website.

Challenge

The Problem

The Solution

Users felt lost and confused navigating through MEP’s website

Clarifying the MEP’s mission and purpos, overhauling the homepage, and updating the navigation

Research

People are interested in donation and volunteer opportunities equally. It is important to make this information readily available. People prefer to donate directly through a nonprofit’s website. It is important to make the page easy to find and trustworthy.

Overall, users found navigating through the site confusing and struggled finding key pages, such as ‘contact us’, ‘donate’, and membership info. Users felt the green color was a little overwhelming and the pages were very text-heavy. Users had some difficulty understanding icons, especially transportation. Users found some of the page titles confusing and weren’t sure what type of content would be there.

Iterations

Wireframing

Prioritize donation visibility, condensed and reprioritized nav with added quick links, clearly linked volunteer opportunities, content chunking, and reformatting content for mobile

Usability Testing

We found the users were able to more successfully navigate through this version of the site. Though there was some confusion when it came to the ‘quick links’ on the homepage, since we had used similar names in the navigation but were showing different options. We also found that users were still feeling overwhelmed by the amount of text on the page, especially when it came to mobile. Lastly, we discovered some navigational buttons we had missed, such as programing the ‘back’ or ‘home’ buttons.

Prototype Updates

We changed the title on the homepage quick links, created expandable sections on mobile, used color blocking and imagery to breakup pages that had a lot of content and added the ‘Donate’ button to the navigation so users can access it from any page.
